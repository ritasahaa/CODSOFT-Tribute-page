A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Great Indian scientist and politician who played a leading role in the development of India's missile and nuclear weapons programs.

About the Legend

A.P.J. Abdul kalam,in full Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul kalam,was born on October 15,1931,in Rameshwaram,Tamil Nadu,India.

He served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007.

Kalam earned a degree in aeronautical engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology and in 1958 joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation(DRDO).

In 1969 ,he moved to the Indian Space Research Organisation,where he was project director of the SLV-III,the first satellite launch vehicle that was both desined and produced in India

Rejoining DRDO in 1982,Kalam planned the program that produced a number of successful missiles,which helped earn him the nickname "Missile Man"

Among the successes was Agni,India's first intermediate range ballistic missile,which incorporated aspects of the SLV-III and was launced in 1989

He also played a pivotal organizational,technical,and political role in India's Pokhran-III nuclear tests in 1998,the first since original nuclear by India in 1974.

From 1992 to 1997 Kalam was scientific adviser to the defence minister,and he later served as principal scientific adviser (1999-2001) to the government with the rank of cabinet minister.

His promient role in the country's 1998 nuclear weapons tests solidified India as a nuclear power and established Kalam as a national hero,although the test caused great concern in the international community.

Wheeler Island,a national missile test site inn Odisha,was renamed Kalam Island in september 2015.

A prominent road in New Delhi was renamed from Aurangzeb Road to Dr APJ Abdul Kalam Road in August 2015.

In February 2018,scientist from the Botanical Survey of India named a newly found plant species as Drypetes kalamii,in his honour.
